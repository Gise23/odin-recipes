Zucchini Bread

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 cups white sugar
  • 1 spoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups grated zucchini
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts

Moist, flavorful, and just the right amount of sweet, this classic zucchini bread is the perfect way to use up fresh zucchini.

Whether you're baking it for breakfast, a snack, or dessert, this easy recipe delivers a tender crumb with hints of warm spice and a subtle nuttiness. It’s a great way to sneak some veggies into your day—deliciously!

This zucchini bread recipe is a delightful blend of wholesome ingredients and comforting flavors. Grated fresh zucchini adds incredible moisture and a soft, tender texture, while warm spices like cinnamon enhance its cozy, home-baked aroma. The simple preparation makes it a reliable favorite for both beginner and seasoned bakers, and it’s easily adaptable with add-ins like nuts or chocolate chips. Whether served fresh out of the oven or toasted the next day, it’s a satisfying treat any time of day.

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C). Grease and flour two 8x4-inch loaf pans or line them with parchment paper.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, baking powder, baking soda, and ground cinnamon. Set aside.
  3. Beat wet ingredients: In another large bowl, beat the eggs until light and frothy. Add the vegetable oil, sugar, and vanilla extract, and mix well until fully combined.
  4. Combine wet and dry: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring just until incorporated. Do not over-mix.
  5. Gently stir in the grated zucchini and chopped walnuts until evenly distributed throughout the batter.
  6. Pour into pans: Divide the batter evenly between the prepared loaf pans and smooth the tops.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 50 to 60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a loaf comes out clean.
  8. Allow the loaves to cool in the pans for about 10 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

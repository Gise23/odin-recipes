Shakshuka

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2–3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon sweet paprika
  • 5 large eggs
  • ¼ teaspoon chili flakes (optional, for heat)
  • 400 g diced tomatoes
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped, for garnish

Hearty, Spiced Shakshuka with Poached Eggs

Shakshuka is a vibrant and comforting dish of eggs poached in a rich, spiced tomato and pepper sauce. Originating from North Africa and widely embraced across the Middle East, it’s both simple and deeply flavorful—thanks to ingredients like garlic, cumin, paprika, and simmered tomatoes. The eggs cook gently in the sauce until the whites are set but the yolks remain perfectly runny, ready to be scooped up with warm bread.

This one-pan meal is as nourishing as it is beautiful, ideal for breakfast, brunch, or a light dinner. It’s easy to make with pantry staples and can be customized with extras like feta cheese, chili flakes, or fresh herbs. Shakshuka is more than just eggs in tomato sauce—it’s a bold, soulful dish that brings people together around the table, no matter the time of day.

Steps:

  1. Sauté the vegetables:Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or frying pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and bell pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, for about 5–7 minutes until softened.
  2. Add garlic and spices: Stir in the garlic, cumin, paprika, and chili flakes (if using). Cook for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Simmer the sauce: Add the diced tomatoes (with their juices) and season with salt and pepper. Simmer uncovered for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly.
  4. Add the eggs: Use a spoon to make small wells in the sauce and crack an egg into each one. Cover the pan with a lid and cook for 5–8 minutes, until the egg whites are set and the yolks are still runny (or to your preferred doneness).
  5. Remove from heat and sprinkle with chopped parsley or cilantro. Add any optional toppings, then serve hot, straight from the pan, with warm bread for dipping.
